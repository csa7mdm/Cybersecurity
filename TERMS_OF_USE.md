## 4. WiFi Testing - Special Legal Notice

> [!CAUTION]
> **WiFi TESTING CARRIES SIGNIFICANT LEGAL RISKS**

### 4.1 WiFi-Specific Laws

In many jurisdictions, the following activities are **CRIMINAL OFFENSES**:

- **Intercepting WiFi communications** (even unencrypted)
- **Deauthentication attacks** (forcibly disconnecting clients)
- **Packet injection** (sending crafted WiFi frames)
- **WPA/WPA2 cracking** (attempting to recover passwords)
- **Evil twin attacks** (creating fake access points)
- **Man-in-the-middle attacks** on WiFi

### 4.2 WiFi Authorization Requirements

To legally test WiFi networks, you **MUST**:

1. **Own the network** (it's your personal/business network), **OR**
2. **Have written authorization** from the network owner
3. **Understand local laws** regarding WiFi testing
4. **Test only specified networks** in your authorization
5. **Do NOT test** neighbor networks, public WiFi, or other networks

### 4.3 Legal Precedents

Users have been prosecuted for WiFi-related activities in multiple jurisdictions:
- Using neighbors' WiFi without permission (theft of services)
- Intercepting WiFi communications (wiretapping violations)
- Deauthentication attacks (denial of service / computer fraud)

**DO NOT assume that testing your own WiFi network is always legal - check local laws.**
